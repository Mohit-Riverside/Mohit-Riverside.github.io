Fun with Simple Circuits

Making a simple robot!

Here's a detailed guide to help you create a basic robot using two DPDT (Double Pole Double Throw) switches, two motors, a battery, wheels, and a chassis made of sunboard (a type of foam board).

Materials Required

  • Two DPDT switches
  • Two DC motors
  • One battery (suitable for the motors, e.g., 6V or 12V)
  • Four wheels (preferably with hubs that fit the motors)
  • 2ftx2ft Sunboard
  • Wires and connectors
  • Double-sided tape or glue (for mounting)
  • Soldering iron and solder (for connections)

Steps to Build the Robot

1. Prepare the Chassis

  1. Cut the Sunboard: Cut the sunboard into a rectangular or square shape to form the base of your robot. The size should be enough to fit your motors and battery comfortably.
  2. Mount the Motors: Position the motors on the chassis where you want them to be. If the motors have mounting holes, use screws to attach them. If not, you can use double-sided tape or glue.
  3. Attach the Wheels: Fix the wheels to the motor shafts. Ensure they are securely attached and can rotate freely.
